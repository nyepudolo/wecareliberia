What is the Family Literacy Initiative?

The Family Literacy Initiative is making a difference in economically challenged communities by empowering parents to be their children’s first teachers. Active in West Point, Caldwell, Duazon, and Neezoe, the initiative has reached 128 families with tailored training and support.

Transformation & Impact

From scribbling to letter knowledge, story sequencing to spelling names, children are progressing thanks to their parents’ dedication. Parents are gaining skills and confidence to guide their children’s learning journey.

Training & Support

Weekly training sessions and hands-on activities prepare home visitors to deliver the curriculum effectively. This training model ensures consistency and accessibility for all participants.

The Heart of the Initiative: Field Visits

Weekly visits help build trust, monitor progress, and mentor parents. This consistent support has been essential to the program’s success.

Expanding the Reach

Assessing 60 new families across the communities has helped the initiative expand its impact and continue making a positive difference.

Creating Lasting Change

By empowering parents and building stronger communities, the Family Literacy Initiative is creating lasting change.
