About GALI

Founded in 2016, the Girls Accelerated Learning Initiative (GALI) emerged from witnessing a 16-year-old struggling in primary school during a teacher training assessment. The program empowers overage girls in grades 1-3 to progress twice yearly, bridging the education gap. Over the last two years alone, GALI has positively impacted over 2,500 girls. Impressively, 77% advanced by two grades, while 87% acquired essential life skills in hygiene and pregnancy prevention.

This program also provides accelerated learning for overage boys and girls in kindergarten, through teacher training programs, teaching guidebooks and student activities book that build required skills and concepts for grade one in one semester. A digital component using offline RACEL server and tablets with resources that are updated periodically, provides skills and access to a variety of contents in various subjects.

The Challenge

In Liberia’s 2022 Education Sector Plan, late enrollment and overage students were prioritized by the Ministry of Education. Overage girls face a higher risk of dropping out due to early marriage and pregnancy, which contributes to Liberia’s higher illiteracy rates among women compared to men. GALI aims to bridge this gap by empowering girls to reach their age-appropriate classes.

Why GALI Matters

Research shows that a woman who can read is twice as likely to send her children to school. Education opens doors to opportunities, better health care, and informed decision-making. For many girls in Liberia, however, these opportunities are out of reach. GALI strives to provide them with access to knowledge, skills, and empowerment.

Program Activities

  • Monitoring and mentoring 120 primary school teachers who serve as tutors for GALI after-school classes.
  • Distributing textbooks to improve teaching and learning.
  • Providing digital learning materials through Mobile Learning Labs.
  • Running after-school programs for 840 overage girls in grades 1–3 to improve academic performance.
  • Providing snacks and sanitary pads to ensure consistent attendance and participation.

Geographic Coverage

  • Brewerville, Bomi Highway, Upper Montserrado County
  • Careysburg, Kakata Highway, Lower Montserrado County
  • Kakata, Margibi County
  • RIA, Margibi County
  • Tubmanburg, Bomi County
  • Buchanan, Grand Bassa County
