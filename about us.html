Our Story

Established in 1992 amidst Liberia's civil unrest, WE-CARE Foundation emerged as a beacon of hope, aiming to rebuild the nation’s education sector. What began as a humble book chain delivering reading materials during curfews has evolved into a comprehensive educational nonprofit organization. Incorporated in 1994, WE-CARE has steadfastly committed itself to fostering a culture of reading and learning across Liberia.

Our Mission

To strengthen Liberia’s education sector by developing and providing quality reading materials, alongside innovative training and teaching methodologies.

Our Vision

We envision a society where reading children become informed adults, capable of making sound decisions that positively impact their communities and the nation at large.

Our Impact

  • Operates one of Liberia's only public libraries with free daily services.
  • Produced culturally relevant children's books through the Reading Liberia Initiative (with CODE).
  • Delivered home-based literacy through the Family Literacy Initiative Project.
  • Introduced critical thinking and participatory learning into classrooms (with OSI/OSIWA support).
