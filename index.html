HOW IT ALL STARTED

WE-CARE Foundation, a Liberian not-for-profit, was founded in 1992 after a brutal civil war. For 31 years, we’ve operated in 10 of Liberia’s 15 counties, transforming education by publishing children’s books, distributing resources, and establishing libraries. It started with a book chain program where people could take books from WE-CARE and, after reading, share them with others without returning them.

We hoped that as people, especially young people, read, they would be too busy to fight or hurt each other. Today, we continue fostering literacy and community engagement across urban and rural areas.

Our Mission

To strengthen Liberia’s education sector through quality reading materials, creative teacher training, and community-driven initiatives, empowering schools, families, and communities.

Our Goals

WE-CARE Foundation aims to foster a love of books and reading based on the truism: Reading children become reading adults, and reading adults become informed citizens who make sound decisions for social and economic development.

WHAT WE DO

WE-CARE Foundation transforms education across Liberia through innovative programs. We run the WE-CARE Library, one of Liberia’s few public libraries, distribute free books, train teachers and librarians, publish culturally relevant books, and promote foundational literacy. Our initiatives, like story hours for kids and accelerated learning for overage girls, foster a love for reading and empower communities.

Publishing

Publishing- WE-CARE Publishing unit has trained writers, illustrators, book designers, editors and worked with external editors and partners to produce more than 40 titles covering early childhood, primary and secondary level books. These books are high quality and culturally relevant. WE-CARE has also developed ECE eBooks containing regular, sign language and audio versions. WE-CARE works with international consultants on book development.

Early Childhood Education(ECE)

WE-CARE is implementing ECE programs at three levels: school-based, community based and home-based. Activities include training parents to be their children first teachers in both rural and urban low-income communities. More than 950 unique families with out-of-school pre-primary children between the ages of 3 - 5 years, have benefited.
